Dialogue for Staff Women is Nov. 11

\n\n

President Lee C. Bollinger and Provost Nancy Cantor will meet with\nwomen staff members noon–1:30 p.m. Nov. 11 in the Michigan Union\nBallroom for a dialogue session designed to discuss women’s\nconcerns.

\n\n

“This is an opportunity for women to share concerns and\nquestions with President Bollinger and Provost Cantor,” said\nSusan Kaufmann, associate director of the Center for the Education of\nWomen, a co-sponsor of the session.

\n\n

Bollinger asked the Advisory Commission on Women’s Issues to\nconvene the forum with the collaboration of the Commission for Women,\nWomen of Color Task Force, Sexual Assault Prevention and Awareness\nCenter, Family Care Resources Program and the Office of Equity and\nDiversity Services-Human Resources and Affirmative Action.

\n\n

A dialogue session for women faculty members is planned for\nnoon–1:30 Dec. 3 in the Pendleton Room, Michigan Union.

Identity of statistics in science examined

\n\n

C. F. Jeff Wu, professor of statistics, will present\n“Statistics = Data Science” at 4:10 p.m. Nov. 10 in Rackham\nAmphitheater. The lecture, in honor of Wu’s appointment to the\nH. C. Carver Collegiate Professorship in Statistics, will focus on\nthe identity of statistics in science. Contrary to the perception of\nstatistics as tables and figures, Wu characterizes statistical work\nas data modeling, analysis and decision making. He will conclude his\nlecture by proposing that statistics be renamed “data\nscience” and statisticians “data scientists.” For more\ninformation, call 998-6240.

Conference on women in political life is Nov. 19 at League

\n\n

“Women in American Political Life: We’ve Come a Long\nWay—Maybe,” presented by the Gerald R. Ford Foundation and\nthe Gerald R. Ford Library, will be held Nov. 19 at the Michigan\nLeague Ballroom.

\n\n

Ford will host the conference, which provides a forum for\nnationally prominent women to discuss the history of women’s\nentrance into American politics, the progress made in the last\nseveral decades and the prognosis for the future.

\n\n

The conference begins at 9:30 a.m. with Ford introducing keynote\nspeaker Elizabeth Dole. At 10:30 a.m. author/biographer Carl Anthony\nwill moderate a panel with Liz Carpenter, press secretary and staff\ndirector for Lady Bird Johnson; Helen Thomas, White House\ncorrespondent for United Press International; Anne Wexler, former\nassistant to President Jimmy Carter; Martha Keys, former Democratic\nrepresentative of Kansas; and Margaret Heckler, former Republican\nrepresentative of Massachusetts and U.S. ambassador to Ireland.

\n\n

A second panel will convene at 1 p.m. with Senator Kay Bailey\nHutchison, Republican representative from Texas; Dee Dee Myers,\nformer press secretary to President Clinton; Ann Lewis, director of\ncommunications for President Clinton; Rae Evans, president of the\nWashington firm Rae Evans and Associates; Sheila Burke, former chief\nof staff to Senator Dole; and Patricia Ireland, president of NOW. The\nconference concludes at 2:30 p.m. The program is free but tickets are\nrequired. Call the Ford Library, 741-2218.

Clements’ exhibit displays far-reaching revolution

\n\n

“Atlantic Perspectives on American Independence:\n1750–1815,” on display at the Clements Library, provides a\nglimpse into the repercussions of the American Revolution on the rest\nof the world, especially Europe, Africa, the Caribbean and South\nAmerica. The exhibition contains rare artifacts from the\nLibrary’s holdings that include: letters bearing the signatures\nof Benjamin Franklin, George Washington, Thaddeus Kosciuszko, John\nPaul Jones and Thomas Jefferson, as well as relevant political\ncartoons, peace treaties, reports, maps and legal documents.

\n\n

The Clements Library is open 1–4:45 p.m. Mon.–Fri.. For\nmore information, call 764-2347.

‘The Future of the Research University’ series seeks\ntopics, speakers

\n\n

Beginning winter term, the Graduate School will present “The\nFuture of the University,” a lecture series addressing how\n200-year-old academic institutions can continue to play a vital role\nin society. Topics and speakers for this endeavor are being sought\nthrough Dec. 14. Topics of particular interest are innovative\nteaching practices in the schools and colleges, issues surrounding\npromotion and tenure, preparing students for non-academic careers,\nstudents’ changing demographics, and distance learning. Speakers\nwithin the U.S. and internationally are invited. To submit ideas\nand/or speaker names, contact Lynne Dumas, 647-2644 or ledumas@umich.edu.

America Recycles Day is Nov. 14

\n\n

The University is joining thousands of other recyclers across the\ncountry in celebrating America Recycles Day Nov 14. Through a\ncooperative effort of Grounds and Waste Management Services, the\nAthletic Department and Gabriel Richard High School, all the\nrecyclable items left behind after the home football game vs.\nWisconsin will be collected.

\n\n

On average more than 26 tons of trash are left in the stadium\nafter a home game. Cardboard from the concession stands is one of the\nfew categories of trash being recycled. Gabriel Richard will be paid\n3 cents for each plastic souvenir cup its students and their families\nseparate from the trash. Announcements during the Nov. 14 game and\npledge cards at America Recycles Day tables will encourage fans to\nrecycle and buy recycled products.
