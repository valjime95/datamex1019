\n

There are a couple of ways to look things up in Wikipedia: by searching or by browsing.\n

\n
  • If you know the name of an article for which you are looking, simply type it into Wikipedia\'s search box.
    • \n
  • If you would like to look around the encyclopedia to see what is on it, use Wikipedia\'s Contents pages. Lists and indices are examples of contents for a published work, and Wikipedia has many of each, including a complete alphabetical index and indices by category.
    Links to all of Wikipedia\'s main contents pages are presented below, and they in turn link to the more specific pages.
\n

Curated article collections

\n

Overview articles

\n

Overview articles summarize in prose a broad topic like biology, and also have illustrations and links to subtopics like cell biology, biographies like Carl Linnaeus, and other related articles like Human Genome Project.\n

\n
  • Portal:Contents/Overviews lists overview articles from covered fields in a single page.
\n

Outline pages

\n

Outline pages have trees of topics in an outline format, which in turn are linked to further outlines and articles providing more detail. Outlines show how important subtopics relate to each other based on how they are arranged in the tree, and they are useful as a more condensed, non-prose alternative to overview articles.\n

\n
  • Portal:Contents/Outlines is a comprehensive list of "Outline of __" pages, organized by subject. It is itself an outline, that links (almost) exclusively to other outlines.
    • \n
  • Outline of academic disciplines covers subjects studied in college or university, and provides links to prose overview articles and their corresponding outlines.
    • \n
  • Outline of knowledge is the top-level outline, its subject being the broadest one of all. It is the ancestor of all other outlines, and they branch out from it, in successive levels.
\n

Vital articles

\n
Main page: Wikipedia:Vital articles
\n

Vital articles are lists of subjects for which Wikipedia should have corresponding high-quality articles. They serve as centralized watchlists to track the status of Wikipedia\'s most important articles.\n

\n
  • Vital articles level 1 \xe2\x80\x93 10 most important articles
    • \n
  • Vital articles level 2 \xe2\x80\x93 100 most important articles
    • \n
  • Vital articles level 3 \xe2\x80\x93 1,000 most important articles

Reference collections

\n

Wikipedia has several types of pages which provide content in a non-prose form, for reference purposes.\n

\n

List pages

\n

List pages enumerate items of a particular type, such as the List of sovereign states or List of South Africans. Wikipedia has "lists of lists" when there are too many items to fit on a single page, when the items can be sorted in different ways, or as a way of navigating lists on a topic (for example Lists of countries and territories or Lists of people). There are several ways to find lists:\n

\n
  • Portal:Contents/Lists \xe2\x80\x93 A limited single-page collection of lists and lists of lists
    • \n
  • List of lists of lists \xe2\x80\x93 A broad single-page collection of lists of lists
    • \n
  • Category:Lists \xe2\x80\x93 Browse lists comprehensively via the multi-page Wikipedia category system
\n

Timelines

\n

Timelines list events chronologically, sometimes including links to articles with more detail. There are several ways to find timelines:\n

\n
  • List of timelines has a long single-page collection
    • \n
  • Category:Timelines has a comprehensive multi-page collection via the Wikipedia category system
\n

Of particular interest may be:\n

\n
  • List of centuries
    • \n
  • List of decades
    • \n
  • List of historical anniversaries \xe2\x80\x93 e.g. events on January 1 of any year
    • \n
  • 2019 \xe2\x80\x93 major events this year
    • \n
  • Portal:Current events \xe2\x80\x93 featured current events and related project activities
    • \n
  • Deaths in 2019 \xe2\x80\x93 lists notable people who died this year
    • \n
  • Category:Graphical timelines \xe2\x80\x93 graphical timelines in the category and subcategories, arranged alphabetically
\n

Glossaries

\n

Glossaries are lists of terms with definitions. Wikipedia includes hundreds of alphabetical glossaries; they can be found two ways:\n

\n
  • Portal:Contents/Glossaries \xe2\x80\x93 A single-page list of glossaries
    • \n
  • Category:Wikipedia glossaries \xe2\x80\x93 Browse glossaries comprehensively via the Wikipedia category system
\n

Bibliographies

\n

Bibliographies list sources on a given topic, for verification or further reading outside Wikipedia:\n

\n
  • Wikipedia:List of bibliographies is a list of bibliographies
    • \n
  • Category:Wikipedia bibliographies has a complete multi-page listing of bibliographies on Wikipedia
\n

Category:Discographies

\n

Discographies catalog the sound recordings of individual artists or groups.\n

\n
  • Category:Discographies has a complete multi-page listing
\n

Special format collections

\n

Portals

\n

Portals include featured articles, images, news, categories, excerpts of key articles, links to related portals, and to-do lists for editors. There are two ways to find portals:\n

\n
  • Portal:Contents/Portals \xe2\x80\x93 A single-page list of portals
    • \n
  • Category:Portals \xe2\x80\x93 Browse portals comprehensively via the Wikipedia category system
\n

Wikipedia books

\n

Wikipedia books are collections of Wikipedia articles that can be viewed, downloaded, or printed into a book. They provide a roadmap for a course of study in a particular subject.\n

\n
  • Category:Wikipedia books (community books) \xe2\x80\x93 an alphabetical list of the books
    • \n
  • Category:Wikipedia books \xe2\x80\x93 a list of the books, categorized by topic
\n

Spoken articles

\n

Growing collections of Wikipedia articles are starting to become available as spoken word recordings as well.\n

\n
  • Category:Spoken articles \xe2\x80\x93 an organized list of all spoken articles
    • \n
  • Wikipedia:Spoken articles \xe2\x80\x93 some general information about the spoken article technology
\n

Collections of articles

\n

Category system

\n

Wikipedia\'s collection of category pages is a classified index system. It is automatically generated from category tags at the bottoms of articles and most other pages. Nearly all of the articles available so far on the website can be found through these subject indexes.\n

If you are simply looking to browse articles by topic, there are three top-level pages to choose from:\n

\n
  • Category:Main topic classifications \xe2\x80\x93 probably what you are looking for: Arts, History, Technology, etc.
    • \n
  • Portal:Contents/Categories \xe2\x80\x93 a hand-crafted list of first- and second-level topic categories
\n

For biographies, see Category:People.\n

Category:Contents is technically at the top of the category hierarchy, but contains many categories useful to editors but not readers. Special:Categories lists every category alphabetically.\n

\n

Alphabetical lists of articles

\n

Wikipedia\'s alphabetical article indexes\n

\n
  • Special:Allpages lists all of the current pages in Wikipedia.
    • \n
  • Portal:Contents/A\xe2\x80\x93Z index provides an easy way to skip to a particular part of the alphabet in the list of all articles.
    • \n
  • Lists of alphabetical indexes\n
    • Category:Wikipedia indexes \xe2\x80\x93 alphabetical list of topic indexes
      • \n
    • Portal:Contents/Indices \xe2\x80\x93 indexes sorted by topic area
\n
